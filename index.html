# Design System Specification: Law Enforcement Editorial ## 1. Overview & Creative North Star The "Creative North Star" for this design system is **The Sovereign Vault**. In designing for a Firearms Licensing Division, we must move beyond the "utilitarian government form" and toward an experience that feels authoritative, impenetrable, and prestigious. This system rejects the cluttered, line-heavy aesthetic of traditional bureaucracy. Instead, it adopts a **High-End Editorial** approachâ€”utilizing vast negative space, intentional asymmetry, and "tonal layering" to guide the user through complex legal processes with the ease of a luxury digital periodical. The goal is to evoke the feeling of a high-security physical vault: heavy, silent, and impeccably organized. We break the "template" look by layering semi-transparent surfaces and using high-contrast typography scales that command attention without shouting. --- ## 2. Colors Our palette is rooted in deep, atmospheric neutrals, punctuated by "Police Blue" and "Gold" to signify institutional authority. ### Tonal Strategy - **Primary Foundation:** Use `surface` (#131313) for the main canvas. For high-security "inner sanctum" areas, transition to `surface_container_lowest` (#0E0E0E) to create visual "weight." - **Institutional Accents:** `primary_container` (#1B365D) is reserved for structural branding, while `secondary` (#E9C176) acts as our "Gold" standard for calls to action and critical highlights. ### The "No-Line" Rule **Explicit Instruction:** Prohibit the use of 1px solid borders to section content. Traditional lines create visual noise that degrades the premium feel. - Boundaries must be defined solely through background color shifts. - Place a `surface_container_low` card atop a `surface` background to define a zone. - Use the `Spacing Scale` (specifically `8` or `12`) to create "voids" that act as invisible separators. ### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule To prevent the dark theme from feeling flat or "muddy," use **Signature Textures**: - **Hero Transitions:** Apply a subtle linear gradient from `primary_container` (#1B365D) to `surface` (#131313) at a 15-degree angle to create a sense of environmental lighting. - **Glassmorphism:** For floating modals or "Secure Verification" overlays, use `surface_variant` at 60% opacity with a `20px` backdrop blur. This creates a "frosted ballistic glass" effect that maintains context while focusing the user. --- ## 3. Typography The typography system balances the brutalist authority of **Public Sans** with the high-utility clarity of **Inter**. | Level | Token | Font | Size | Weight/Style | | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | | **Display** | `display-lg` | Public Sans | 3.5rem | Bold / Tight Kerning | | **Headline** | `headline-md` | Public Sans | 1.75rem | Semi-Bold | | **Title** | `title-lg` | Inter | 1.375rem | Medium | | **Body** | `body-md` | Inter | 0.875rem | Regular / 1.6 Line Height | | **Label** | `label-md` | Inter | 0.75rem | Bold / All-Caps (Letter-spacing: 0.05em) | **Editorial Intent:** Use `display-lg` for section numbers or high-level status (e.g., "01. APPLICATION"). The contrast between the oversized headers and the functional `body-md` creates an "Official Document" hierarchy that feels intentional and bespoke. --- ## 4. Elevation & Depth In this system, depth is a measure of "Security Clearance." The deeper the user goes into a process, the more "layered" the UI becomes. ### The Layering Principle (Tonal Stacking) Instead of drop shadows, use the surface hierarchy: 1. **Level 0 (Background):** `surface_dim` (#131313) 2. **Level 1 (Sectioning):** `surface_container_low` (#1C1B1B) 3. **Level 2 (Active Cards):** `surface_container` (#201F1F) 4. **Level 3 (Floating Elements):** `surface_bright` (#393939) ### Ambient Shadows Shadows are only permitted for floating action elements (e.g., a "Submit Application" FAB). - **Shadow Specs:** Blur: `24px`, Opacity: `8%`, Color: `primary_fixed_dim` (#AEC7F7). This creates a "blue-tinted glow" rather than a grey smudge, mimicking a high-tech display. ### The "Ghost Border" Fallback If a container requires a border for accessibility (e.g., an input field), use the `outline_variant` token at **15% opacity**. It should be felt, not seen. --- ## 5. Components ### Buttons (High-Impact) - **Primary:** Background: `secondary` (#E9C176); Text: `on_secondary` (#412D00). Use `md` (0.375rem) roundedness. - **Secondary:** Background: `transparent`; Border: `outline_variant` (20% opacity); Text: `secondary`. - **States:** On hover, apply a `secondary_container` (#604403) soft glow behind the button. ### Input Fields (The "Dossier" Style) - Fields should not be boxes. Use a "bottom-line-only" approach or a very subtle `surface_container_highest` background. - **Active State:** The bottom border transitions to `secondary` (Gold) with a `2px` height. - **Helper Text:** Use `label-sm` in `on_surface_variant` (#C4C6CF). ### Cards & Lists (The "Vault" Grid) - **Strictly No Dividers.** To separate list items (e.g., "Firearm Inventory"), use a `2px` vertical gap and alternate backgrounds between `surface_container_low` and `surface_container_lowest`. - **Interaction:** On hover, a card should shift from `surface_container` to `surface_container_high` and move `2px` to the right, creating a "mechanical drawer" feel. ### Critical Status Indicators - **Success:** `surface_container_high` background with a `secondary_fixed_dim` (Gold) left-accent bar. - **Error:** `error_container` (#93000A) background with `on_error_container` (#FFDAD6) text. --- ## 6. Do's and Don'ts ### Do: - **Do** use `24` (6rem) or `20` (5rem) spacing between major sections to let the design breathe. - **Do** use `surface_tint` at low opacities (3-5%) as an overlay on images to pull them into the "Police Blue" brand world. - **Do** use asymmetry. For example, a right-aligned "Apply Now" button offset against a left-aligned display header. ### Don't: - **Don't** use 100% white (#FFFFFF) for body text. Use `on_surface_variant` (#C4C6CF) to reduce eye strain in dark mode. - **Don't** use standard "drop shadows" with black or grey colors. - **Don't** use fully rounded (pill-shaped) buttons unless they are small action chips. Stick to `md` (0.375rem) for an authoritative, "squared-off" look. - **Don't** use "Alert Red" for anything other than a destructive action or a legal violation.
